二、误杀根因分析

精度 0% too_professional_or_medical 70条全部误杀 整条删除

根因：规则用「临床医疗」的标准，误伤了大量运动医学/运动康复/运动科学内容。 这些内容的话题是「肩袖/髌骨/耳压平衡/皮质醇」，语言听起来医学，但本质是运动伤病管理和运动生理， 属于我们内容池核心价值。

结论：运动教学内容池里，「运动生理+运动伤病+运动康复」三类内容永远是合法的，不存在「过于医疗」这条线。 真正需要排除的是无运动背景的纯临床（如化疗方案、药品用法），而这类内容本就不可能进入运动视频池。 → 直接删除此规则。

❌ 被误杀的运动医学内容（应保留）

3 at-home decompression moves to relieve shoulder impingement and arthritis pain
→ 肩关节运动康复，理所应当保留
Why most rotator cuff pain doesn't actually start at the rotator cuff
→ 运动损伤机理，核心内容
Why resurfacing too fast can kill a diver: how decompression sickness forms
→ 潜水运动安全/生理，保留
A qigong breathwork routine that uses movement to break up excess mucus
→ 气功呼吸练习，保留
Why cortisone injections are the wrong call for chronic tennis elbow
→ 网球肘处理，运动伤病，保留

真正应该剔除的医疗内容（几乎不会出现）

How to dose metformin for type 2 diabetes → 纯临床用药，不可能出现在运动池
Chemotherapy regimen for lung cancer → 与运动无关的临床治疗

此类内容在运动视频采集环节即已过滤，无需在topic质检层再设此规则。

精度 0% exaggerated_or_factual_risk 16条全部误杀 整条删除

根因：LLM 将「有争议的运动科学观点」和「反常识的技术建议」当成「事实错误」处理。 但这恰恰是优质运动内容最常见的切入角度——「你以为X，但其实Y」的结构本身就是好内容。 LLM 没有能力区分「反常识但正确的运动知识」和「真正危险的误导」。

结论：topic 层不应该判断内容是否「科学正确」，这是视频本身的责任。→ 直接删除此规则。

❌ 被误杀的合理运动观点（应保留）

Why long-distance runners can skip water mid-run if they pre-hydrate correctly
→ 争议性但合理的运动水分观点
How a beginner can land a solo backflip in a single coaching session
→ 夸张吸睛，但是合法的training内容
Why beginner mountain bikers should ease off the front brake
→ 技术建议，非误导
How a specific breathing technique can raise your body's natural EPO levels
→ 运动生理话题，争议性学术观点
Why being left-handed makes catching in baseball structurally impossible
→ 棒球规则讨论，合法内容

真正危险的误导（极罕见）

Take 50mg cocaine before sprinting to run faster → 明显危险建议
Never wear helmets when mountain biking, they cause injuries → 反安全常识

此类极端案例在采集环节已过滤，topic质检层无需此规则。

精度 28.6% other 91条中65条误杀，26条正确 重大收紧：明确白名单

根因：LLM 对「什么算运动」的认知太狭窄，将以下内容全部错误归类为「非运动」： 摩托车运动、气功/太极、自行车维修、登山/徒步/露营、潜水、舞蹈、自卫术、户外野生安全。 实际上这些都是我们内容池需要的内容类型。

v3 System Prompt（已更新至 run_qc_12k.py）

SYSTEM_PROMPT v3 — Rules: 1. sport_unspecified — Topic uses generic sport verbs/nouns WITHOUT naming the sport: e.g. "the swing", "the spin move", "players", "the drill", "the kick", "the pass" Only flag if NO specific sport name exists anywhere in the topic text. ✓ OK: "the swing in golf", "basketball spin move" ✗ Flag: "Why beginners stop thinking about the swing" — which sport? 2. too_many_items — Topic explicitly lists ≥8 distinct items/techniques/moves. NEVER flag: training durations ("7-day plan"), performance numbers ("180 steps/min") ONLY flag: "19-technique boxing combo", "11 movements", "15 paddles tested" 3. person_too_narrow — ONLY flag completely anonymous unknown individuals as sole subject. NEVER flag recognizable athletes, coaches, teams, or public figures. 4. unclear_value — Topic contains ZERO identifiable skill/action/technique/sport noun. NEVER flag if any specific movement, technique, or sport is named. 5. empty_or_no_value — Pure click-bait with no hint of what will be learned. 6. other — Clearly NOT sports/fitness/outdoor activity. NEVER flag (these ARE sports/fitness): - Motorcycle/motorbike sports and riding technique - Qigong, tai chi, breathwork, meditation, yoga (all forms), face yoga - Cycling technique AND equipment maintenance/repair (brakes, cables, parts) - Hiking, backpacking, mountaineering, camping, trail navigation - Scuba diving, cave diving, freediving, snorkeling, shark diving - Dance (all forms: house, bachata, contemporary, ballet, etc.) - Self-defense, martial arts, weapon drills (butterfly knife, etc.) - Outdoor survival skills (navigation, wilderness first aid, bear safety) - Golf course knowledge, sports venue knowledge - Sports nutrition, sports medicine, sports rehabilitation, sports science - Any physical activity requiring technique or training ONLY flag: pure photography/videography, video games, cooking (no sports context), finance, home repair, purely spiritual/religious (no fitness tie-in) 7. mixed_unrelated_points — Combines completely unrelated topics in one confusing topic. Only flag if topics are truly unrelated (not just multi-faceted coverage of one sport). REMOVED RULES (no longer used): - too_professional_or_medical: 0% precision over 2 rounds; sports medicine/rehab is ALWAYS kept - exaggerated_or_factual_risk: 0% precision; LLM cannot distinguish debatable sports science from genuinely dangerous misinformation; this judgment belongs at content level, not topic level
